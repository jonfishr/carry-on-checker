How This Tool Works

Type in your bag's height, width, and depth (including wheels and handles!) and this page compares them against each airline's published carry-on limit. You'll get one of three answers for every airline:

  • ✅ Fits — your bag is within the airline's published size limit.
  • ⚠️ Close call — your bag is over the limit, but by no more than 2.5 cm (1 inch). In Aly's real-world testing, bags this close usually slide into the sizer just fine.
  • ❌ Too big — more than 2.5 cm over on at least one side. Expect trouble at the gate.

Why "close call" exists

Published limits are stricter than real life. For example, the Monos Carry-On measures 55.88 cm tall while Air Canada's sizer says 55 cm — technically too tall, but it fits in the actual sizer bin with ease, and Aly has flown with it on Air Canada many times. That's exactly the kind of nuance a plain checklist misses.

Not all dimensions matter equally

There's a hierarchy when it comes to carry-on sizing: height matters most — it's the first thing that keeps a bag out of a sizer or overhead bin. Width comes next, and depth matters least, especially on soft-sided bags that squish. If you're over on depth only, your odds are better than being over on height.

Where the data comes from

The size limits come from each airline's official baggage page, based on the dataset Aly Smalls built for her Carry-On Compliance Checker spreadsheet. Each airline shows when it was last checked, with a link to the official source.

Please note: airlines can change their rules, and enforcement can vary. This tool is here to help you plan with confidence — not add stress. When in doubt, double-check with your airline before you fly.
